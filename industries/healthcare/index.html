The Operating Reality

Three forces reshaping healthcare technology

Every healthcare organization today is navigating the same structural pressures. Solving them requires a partner with domain depth, not just technical headcount.

Legacy Infrastructure Blocking Care Delivery

A typical health system runs 40 or more distinct technology platforms. Aging EHR, RCM, and scheduling systems prevent coordinated, data-driven care while regulatory requirements grow more stringent each cycle. The debt compounds faster than most IT organizations can address it.

Interoperability Gaps Across the Value Chain

FHIR, HL7, DICOM, SNOMED CT, LOINC — the regulatory interoperability mandate is clear, but execution requires both technical precision and healthcare domain knowledge. Data silos between Payors, Providers, and Pharma organizations cost the industry billions in rework and delayed care decisions annually.

Workforce Gaps in a Specialized Field

Healthcare IT demands a rare combination of clinical domain knowledge and technology expertise. Pre-authorization nurses, Epic/Cerner specialists, clinical trial managers, and revenue cycle experts are not interchangeable with general IT talent. Building and deploying this workforce at speed requires a dedicated pipeline.
